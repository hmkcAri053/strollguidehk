Places of Interest

Nam Kok Rd

Nam Kok Road preserves the soul of Kowloon City. Though the Walled City is gone, its cultural warmth still lingers in these streets. The area is lined with authentic Thai eateries — from spicy tom yum soup to sweet mango sticky rice — each bite offering a glimpse of the city’s rich and lively heritage.

Carpenter Road Park

Carpenter Road Park offers a refreshing pocket of calm in the city. Its open lawns blend seamlessly with gentle cycling paths, creating a relaxed rhythm of movement and rest. Locals read beneath the trees, children play on the grass, and cyclists glide through dappled sunlight. Though only steps away from Kowloon City’s bustle, the park carries a soothing energy — a perfect spot to breathe, unwind, and feel slower pace of life.

Kowloon Walled City Park

Rising from the ruins of the former Walled City, this park blends Lingnan-style garden design with traces of history. The once-crowded buildings are gone, replaced by lush trees, winding paths, and elegant pavilions that preserve the spirit of the old city. Walking through its quiet greenery, you can still sense echoes of the world that once stood here.
